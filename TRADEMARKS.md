## Why this exists

N-PAMP is an open protocol. Anyone may implement it, build relays, routers, and
tools for it, and say so. The trademark does not restrict that and is not meant
to. Its only purpose is to keep the *names* "N-PAMP" and "BubbleFish" as
reliable signals of origin — so that "N-PAMP" continues to mean the
specification published here.

## Uses that are always fine (no permission needed)

You may use the names truthfully to refer to the protocol and its origin,
including:

- stating that your software **implements**, **is compatible with**, or
  **works with** N-PAMP;
- referring to "an N-PAMP relay," "an N-PAMP implementation," or "the N-PAMP
  specification";
- accurate, descriptive references in documentation, articles, talks, and
  academic work.

The test: you may use the names to describe what something *is* or what it
*interoperates with*, as long as you do not imply that BubbleFish Technologies
produces, sponsors, endorses, or certifies it.

## Uses that need written permission

- Using "N-PAMP" or "BubbleFish" (or confusingly similar names) **in the name of
  your own product, company, service, domain, or logo**.
- Any use suggesting **affiliation, sponsorship, endorsement, or
  official/certified status** (e.g., "official N-PAMP").
- Using the marks for a **modified or forked** protocol in a way that presents
  it as N-PAMP itself. Forks should adopt their own name.
- Altering the marks (spelling, spacing, hyphenation) or using them in your own
  branding as a verb or in the plural.
